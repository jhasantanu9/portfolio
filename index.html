Some Projects I Have Worked On.

Google Data Analytics Capstone Project: Cyclistic Project Link

Bike Share Analysis Report

Summary:
In this analysis, I explored Cyclistic's bike-share program, dissecting user behavior between annual members and casual riders in Chicago. Leveraging historical trip data, I meticulously cleaned and manipulated the dataset to ensure accuracy in my findings. The analysis section covers a range of dimensions, from member type distribution based on trip duration to day-of-the-week and time-of-day patterns. Visualizations aid in conveying insights effectively. The recommendations provided focus on actionable strategies like targeted promotions and service optimization to enhance the overall user experience. This report serves as a testament to my data science skills and strategic thinking, offering valuable insights for stakeholders in the bike-sharing industry.

Google Data Analytics Capstone Project: Bella Beat Project Link

BellaBeat Analysis Report

Summary:
In this Google Data Analytics Capstone Project, I analyzed FitBit tracker data to inform Bellabeat's marketing strategy, specifically for their Time watch. Key findings include high usage of activity tracking, a popular sleep feature, and low engagement with weight tracking. Recommendations emphasize improving weight tracking usability, enhancing sleep feature comfort, and prioritizing marketing for the activity tracker. This project showcases my data analysis skills in providing actionable insights for business strategy.

Cannabis Retail Sales Dashboard of Connecticut State Project Link

A Comprehensive Dashboard

Summary:
This project presents a detailed analysis of cannabis retail sales in Connecticut, focusing on both adult-use cannabis and medical marijuana markets. The analysis covers data from January 2023 to June 2024 and includes visualizations of total retail sales, the number of products sold, and average product prices. The dashboard provides valuable insights into market trends, helping stakeholders make informed decisions about market strategies, regulatory policies, and investment opportunities. The data, sourced from the Connecticut Health and Human Services department, is visualized using Tableau, with preprocessing and analysis conducted in Excel.
